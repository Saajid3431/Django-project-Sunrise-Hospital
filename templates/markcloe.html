Shoulder and Upper Limb Surgery

Dr. Mark Cloe

Dr. Mark Cloe is a renowned shoulder and upper limb surgeon based in London. He has extensive experience and knowledge in treating various injuries and conditions affecting the shoulder, elbow, wrist and hand. He is skilled in performing arthroscopic, reconstructive and prosthetic surgery, as well as microsurgery and tissue engineering. He is also an innovator in the field of bionic devices that can enhance nerve function and movement. He is passionate about helping his patients recover and improve their quality of life. He is a compassionate and dedicated doctor who always strives for excellence.

Treatments



Arthroscopic surgery for repairing damaged cartilage, ligaments, tendons and bones in the knee and shoulder joints.

Joint replacement surgery for replacing worn-out or diseased knee and shoulder joints with artificial implants.

Amputation surgery for removing a part of an arm or leg that is severely damaged or infected by cancer or other diseases.

Rehabilitation and physiotherapy for restoring function, mobility and strength after surgery or injury.

Experience

Dr.Mark Cloe has performed thousands of surgeries on patients with various shoulder and upper limb problems, such as fractures, dislocations, arthritis, rotator cuff tears, nerve injuries, infections, tumors and congenital deformities. He is proficient in using the latest techniques and technologies, such as arthroscopy, joint replacement, bone grafting, nerve repair, tendon transfer and muscle flap. He is also one of the pioneers in developing and applying bionic devices that can stimulate the nerves and restore some function and sensation to the paralyzed limbs.

Awards

He is affiliated with UM Upper Chesapeake Health and accepts new patients and telehealth appointments. He has received the following awards for his outstanding work in the field of upper limb surgery:

1. Board of Directors Award for being a member of the board of directors of Wrightington Hospital for five years.

2. Liverpool Upper Limb Unit Award for his continuous evaluation of the service and involvement in clinical research.

3. PR Training Program Award for showing exceptional performance in the PR training program.
